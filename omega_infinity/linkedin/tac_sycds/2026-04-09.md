A useful evidence alignment topology is partial convergence across mechanistic, genetic, and clinical structure.

In this topology, mechanistic evidence establishes causal plausibility, genetic evidence reinforces directional validity, and clinical evidence remains incomplete or underpowered. The key criterion is not whether two layers agree. The key criterion is whether the full stack supports transfer across layers without unresolved structural gaps.

Evaluation should remain criteria-oriented:

- mechanistic structure should specify a coherent causal pathway
- genetic structure should reinforce or weaken target-level alignment
- clinical structure should determine whether the signal closes at the human outcome layer
- uncertainty should be preserved when one layer remains incomplete

Under this topology, lower-layer alignment is informative but not sufficient for strong advancement confidence. Evidence alignment is therefore conditional on stack closure rather than directional consistency alone.
