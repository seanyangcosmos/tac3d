One evidence alignment topology is partial-stack convergence across mechanistic, genetic, and clinical structure.

In this topology, mechanistic evidence establishes causal plausibility, genetic evidence supports target-level alignment, and clinical evidence remains incomplete, uncertain, or not yet outcome-resolving. The decision boundary depends on whether the stack closes across all three layers rather than whether lower layers point in the same direction.

Criteria should remain explicit.

- mechanistic structure should identify a coherent causal pathway rather than isolated association
- genetic structure should strengthen or weaken alignment at the target layer
- clinical structure should determine whether transfer is supported at the human outcome layer
- unresolved clinical structure should prevent strong advancement claims even when lower layers converge

Under this topology, lower-layer agreement is informative but not sufficient. Alignment should therefore be treated as conditional on cross-layer closure, not directional consistency alone.
