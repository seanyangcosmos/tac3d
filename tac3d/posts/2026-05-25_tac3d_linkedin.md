TAC-3D — Chiesi–KalVista (EKTERLY/sebetralstat; hereditary angioedema)
Verdict: ELIGIBLE
Scores: Eligibility Index 76 | Tension 85 | Alignment 72 | Convergence 70

Structure first. The plasma kallikrein → bradykinin axis is the causal engine in bradykinin‑mediated HAE attacks; an on‑demand oral kallikrein inhibitor cleanly intersects that pathology. Genetics (SERPING1 in classic HAE; F12 and related contact‑pathway variants in some normal‑C1‑INH HAE) reinforce the target rationale by anchoring disease biology in dysregulated kallikrein–kinin activity.

Clinical maturity is high. Phase 3 KONFIDENT met endpoints with clear separation in time to symptom relief, and FDA approval (EKTERLY) closes the translational loop from mechanism to human effect. That tri‑layer coherence pushes the decision into “eligible.”

The gate is convergence under competition and execution. Oral on‑demand must coexist with established oral prophylaxis (e.g., berotralstat) and with injectable prophylaxis options; differentiation and positioning matter. BioCryst’s long‑acting plasma kallikrein candidate and new entries from major players add timing pressure. Integration also needs attention as KalVista’s focused HAE team meets a broader rare‑disease portfolio.

Implications: treat confidence as routed through the stack. Maintain the mechanistic and genetic clarity, but manage convergence by (1) defining a crisp oral on‑demand value story distinct from oral prophylaxis, (2) tracking competitor timelines closely, and (3) executing a thoughtful integration plan that preserves HAE expertise. With those conditions, the acquisition thesis remains structurally sound.
