TAC-3D verdict: ELIGIBLE (Eligibility Index 78 | Tension 78 | Alignment 82 | Convergence 73)

The bezuclastinib + sunitinib result in PEAK (2L GIST) advances from partial-stack promise to cross-layer eligibility. Mechanistically, adding A‑loop coverage (bezuclastinib; KIT exon17/18 potency) to ATP‑pocket activity (sunitinib) addresses the polyclonal resistance seen after imatinib. Genetically, KIT remains the dominant driver in GIST and secondary mutations distribute across ATP-pocket and activation-loop loci. This coupling hypothesis is validated clinically: PEAK shows PFS 16.5 vs 9.2 months (HR 0.50) with higher ORR (46% vs 26%) and consistency across KIT mutation subgroups. The FDA’s Priority Review (PDUFA Nov 30, 2026) further reduces time-to-decision risk.

Why not “validated” yet? Uncertainty still propagates from the human layer. Overall survival is immature; long-term combination safety (notably hepatic events) warrants monitoring; and approval/commercial execution remain gating realities. These are tensions to track, not reasons to ignore the structural advance.

What to watch next: (1) PDUFA outcome and any FDA requests, (2) OS maturation and postmarketing safety signals, (3) launch discipline versus sunitinib standard and evolving competition (ripretinib, avapritinib), and (4) market access dynamics.

Conclusion: Strong cross-evidence compatibility plus robust Phase 3 efficacy and regulatory momentum justify ELIGIBLE status. Treat the next milestones as boundary checks rather than thesis re‑writes.
