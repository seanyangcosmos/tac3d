Topology is a discipline of boundary conditions. Good decisions come from respecting what each layer can and cannot tell you. Most failure modes are boundary violations dressed up as confidence.

At the core, represent the question and evidence as structure, not prose. Then force cross‑evidence arbitration: does the mechanism cohere, is there genetic reinforcement, does the human layer actually close the loop? This is where uncertainty stops being hand‑waved and starts being budgeted.

Don’t let local certainty escape its container. Mechanistic elegance without human closure is still constrained. A persuasive clinical read‑out without mechanistic or genetic scaffolding often collapses under transfer. Confidence does not freely propagate; it is gated at every interface.

Only after the structure is settled should interpretation begin. The overlay is not a court of appeals for inconvenient gaps. Its job is to render a decision object faithfully, preserving the tensions that remain, so strategy carries the right amount of doubt.

Simple rule of thumb: Core (build structure) → Decision (arbitrate across layers) → Overlay (communicate). Keep the order, protect the boundaries, and your narratives will stay honest about what is known, what is inferred, and what is still open.
