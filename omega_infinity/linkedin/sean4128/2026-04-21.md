A common way good science goes sideways is by optimizing for movement rather than closure. Teams pick endpoints that are easy to shift inside a layer, then speak as if the stack has changed state. The signal looks alive, but the gate above is still open.

Topology treats transfer as gated. Confidence is local until the next layer’s closure criteria are satisfied. That means some attractive metrics are structurally weak. they describe behavior inside a model or cohort but do not bind the outcome that the next layer demands. When those metrics improve, interpretation improves locally, not globally.

Asymmetry makes this worse. Different layers have different error surfaces and observability. A robust shift in a surrogate can be fragile when translated to function. A clean functional read can be brittle when translated to adoption. The map is not pessimistic. it is protective. It forces measurement to earn the right to propagate.

Decision discipline follows from this. Choose the smallest endpoint that closes the current gate under the hardest skeptic. Publish its minimal clinically or operationally important difference. Tie resources to resolving designs, not expressive ones. Let interpretation trail structure by design. That is how movement becomes closure rather than another partial-stack convergence.
