TAC-3D verdict: ELIGIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS — Eligibility Index 57 (Tension 63, Alignment 52, Convergence 58)

Merck’s MK-2010 (PD-1/VEGF bispecific) posted early ORR signals in NSCLC — 55% in 1L and 44% in later lines — that benchmark competitively versus ivonescimab. Structurally, the plan is viable but gated. The current ORR-centric story is misaligned with survival-driven regulatory endpoints and with a competitive landscape where Summit/Akeso already have a November FDA decision date.

Cross-layer readout:
- Mechanistic: Immune–angiogenic synergy is plausible and supported by prior precedents. Overlapping toxicities and context-dependent translation remain real risks.
- Clinical: No survival readouts yet. To be meaningfully competitive, ORR must translate into PFS and OS in randomized, later-stage studies.
- Strategy: The competitive window is narrowing. Differentiation that leans only on ORR will underperform. Portfolio options (e.g., ADC combinations) and biomarker-driven selection offer clearer edges.

Conditions to proceed:
- Define and launch a survival-first Phase 2/3 strategy within 6 months, with timelines and endpoints locked.
- Publish a concrete differentiation path beyond ORR — either a combo strategy or a biomarker program tied to survival.
- Tighten partner coordination to accelerate registrational starts and de-risk supply.

Bottom line: The decision is eligible with conditions. Converting attractive ORR into survival, fast, and with a distinct strategic edge is the difference between catching the wave and watching it pass.
