TAC-3D verdict: ELIGIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS — Eligibility Index 70 (Tension 72, Alignment 66, Convergence 73)

Regeneron’s Otarmeni (lunsotogene parvec) is now FDA‑approved for OTOF‑related hearing loss. The pivotal study reported 80% of children reached moderate‑to‑normal hearing by 6 months, with mainly procedural/ear‑related adverse events. Label includes adults. Launch is structurally viable if durability is confirmed and operations scale in order.

Cross‑layer readout:
- Clinical: Approval anchored to compelling 6‑month efficacy; durability beyond 6–12 months remains to be proven in confirmatory follow‑up.
- Access: Drug provided at no cost to eligible US patients, but facility/administration costs persist; genetic testing + referral pathway required; very rare population further narrowed by cochlear implant constraints.
- Operations: Surgical inner‑ear delivery requires specialized teams, OR time, peri‑op protocols, and long‑term audiology/rehab. Center‑of‑excellence buildout, surgeon training/certification, and registry infrastructure are gating items.

Conditions to de‑risk and scale:
- Define COE requirements/timeline and launch an accredited surgeon training program.
- Establish an RWE registry with durability and safety benchmarks; tie scaling to confirmatory milestones.
- Build payer strategy for administration costs and care pathway coverage; pre‑wire prior auth criteria.
- Stand up genetic testing + early referral pipeline to find candidates efficiently.

Bottom line: Clinically compelling and now eligible to launch, but confidence must propagate through durability evidence and operational gates to achieve sustainable access and outcomes.
