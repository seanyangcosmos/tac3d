TAC-3D verdict: ELIGIBLE_WITH_CONDITIONS — Eligibility Index 66 (Tension 73, Alignment 65, Convergence 60)

Avalyn is aiming to raise ~$182M to fund Phase 3 programs for inhaled reformulations in IPF/respiratory disease. The strategy is structurally viable but gated. The core assumption is that inhaled delivery can improve tolerability and persistence enough to drive adoption versus entrenched orals (pirfenidone, nintedanib). That must be proven alongside clinically meaningful effects on progression (e.g., FVC decline, exacerbations).

Cross‑layer readout:
- Clinical: Prior tolerability signals are encouraging, but Phase 3 still needs a clear FVC/progression benefit; deposition variability and chronic inhalation safety are non‑trivial.
- Market: Physician inertia and payer step therapy favor status quo unless Avalyn demonstrates fewer discontinuations and better persistence translating to outcomes/total‑cost gains.
- Regulatory/CMC: Reformulation may leverage 505(b)(2) elements, but device/CMC readiness and Phase 3 outcomes are still decisive. Execution risk rises if programs run in parallel without sequencing.

Conditions to proceed:
- Lock a compelling Phase 2b/lead‑in signal that de‑risks FVC/progression before full Phase 3 spend.
- Build a payer value proposition around discontinuation reduction, persistence, and QoL—supported by robust HEOR.
- Set device/CMC milestones (manufacturing scale‑up, in‑use performance, training logistics) aligned to clinical timelines.
- Adopt risk‑adjusted sequencing if cash cannot support parallel pivotal trials to full readout.

Bottom line: Eligible with conditions—prove differentiation clinically, make the payer case explicit, and sequence execution to avoid overextending before decisive data.
