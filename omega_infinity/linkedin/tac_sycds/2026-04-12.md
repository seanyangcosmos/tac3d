One evidence alignment topology is ordered cross-layer evaluation across mechanistic, genetic, and clinical structure.

In this topology, mechanistic structure establishes causal plausibility, genetic structure refines target-level alignment, and clinical structure determines whether the signal resolves at the human outcome layer. The key constraint is that these layers should not be treated as interchangeable. Each contributes a distinct criterion to the final decision boundary.

Evaluation should remain criteria-oriented.

- mechanistic structure should establish coherent causal linkage
- genetic structure should strengthen or weaken target relevance
- clinical structure should determine whether transfer is supported in human outcomes
- unresolved structure in any one layer should preserve uncertainty at the decision boundary

Under this topology, confidence should propagate only when the stack remains ordered and each layer closes its own role. Directional support alone is not sufficient if the sequence of validation remains incomplete.
