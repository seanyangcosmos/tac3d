One evidence alignment topology is cross-layer partial convergence with unresolved clinical transfer.

In this topology, mechanistic structure establishes causal plausibility, genetic structure supports target-level alignment, and clinical structure remains incomplete or insufficiently outcome-resolving. The key criterion is not whether upstream layers agree. The key criterion is whether the evidence stack closes at the human outcome layer without a remaining transfer gap.

Evaluation should remain explicit.

- mechanistic structure should show a coherent causal pathway rather than isolated descriptive signal
- genetic structure should reinforce or weaken target-level support
- clinical structure should determine whether the proposed effect resolves at the human layer
- incomplete clinical structure should preserve uncertainty even when mechanistic and genetic layers align

Under this topology, lower-layer convergence is informative but not sufficient for strong advancement confidence. Alignment should therefore be judged by cross-layer closure rather than directional agreement alone.
