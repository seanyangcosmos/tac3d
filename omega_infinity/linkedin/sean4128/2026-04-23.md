Progress without falsifiers is choreography. It looks coordinated, but no one is actually trying to knock the structure down. If your plan cannot say what would make you stop at the current gate, you are not doing science. You are rehearsing a story.

Topology makes falsifiers mandatory. A resolving endpoint without a predeclared falsifier invites motivated interpretation. The moment results arrive, claims bend around them. When the falsifier is part of the design, the gate either closes or it does not. Confidence stops being a vibe and becomes a permission earned in order.

Asymmetry makes this discipline more important, not less. Some measures are easy to move inside a layer and nearly orthogonal to the next. Others are hard to move but bind the decision above. The job is to pick the smallest measurement that will change the next gate’s state under the hardest skeptic, then make everything else subordinate to it. If a metric can improve without changing the decision, it belongs in the supporting cast.

Operationally this looks unglamorous. Define the resolving endpoint and its minimal important difference. Write the falsifier in plain language. Design the smallest study that can flip the gate with acceptable error. Commit to stopping rules. Let interpretation trail structure by design. You will ship fewer slides and more outcomes, because you are measuring what decides rather than what performs.
